  • Polyfills patch the technology that we expect a browser to provide natively.

    What is a Polyfill?

  • Plugins are components that add specific capabilities to a parent web application.

  • Responsive Web Design is a design process that allows our sites to adapt to the media that renders them.

It seems to me (@iamandyjones) that in the world of front-end web development there are more (buzz) words describing different concepts, processes and trends than ever before. This site aims to clarify the meaning behind some of them, and hopefully prove useful to anyone scratching their head about current web design hot topics.

N.B. The links referenced on this site are from my own bookmark collections, and are those which I consider to be favourites. The buzzword definitions given are what I feel best describe the terms, but the authors of the articles included may or may not agree. It's likely the authors themselves have never seen this site.

Front-end web development is in a rapid phase of progression, and it's likely some people may disagree with some of what I've noted down. That's a good thing, and I'm all ears.
